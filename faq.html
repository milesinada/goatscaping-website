Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you wanted to know about goatscaping on Kauai

About the Goats

Goats love weeds, grass, brush, vines, brambles, and most invasive plants. They especially enjoy Guinea grass, blackberry, lantana, young guava, and many common Kauai invasives. They eat leaves, stems, and bark, often targeting the plants you most want gone.
Goats generally avoid plants that are toxic to them, they have good instincts. They also typically won't eat mature tree trunks, most ornamental plants, or plants you want to keep. We always do a walkthrough first to identify what you want protected so we can fence those areas off.
It depends on the job size. Small residential jobs typically use a small herd of 4–8 goats. Larger properties may call for 12–20 or more. We scale the herd to match the work.

Safety & Your Family

Yes! Our goats are friendly and accustomed to people. Kids absolutely love them. We do ask that children are supervised around the goats, and that they don't feed them without asking us first, some human foods aren't good for goats.
We ask that dogs are kept away from the grazing area during work. Most dogs are curious and excitable around goats, which can stress the herd and disrupt their grazing. We'll set up a clear boundary and ask that dogs stay on the other side while the goats are working.
We use portable electric netting fencing to create a managed grazing zone. The goats stay within this area and we move it as sections are cleared. We've never had an escapee, these animals know their routine.
Not necessarily. We're on-site to manage the herd, but you don't need to babysit us. We check in with you at the start and end of each day. Many clients just go about their normal routine.

The Process
